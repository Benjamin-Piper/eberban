Core grammar

Relations and predicates

The language is made of 2 kinds of things : relation and predicates. Relations describes the interaction between some entities, while a predicate asserts the relation between the provided entities is true. In the english sentence "I eat an apple", there is the relation of "eating", with the subject being "I" and the thing being eaten is "an apple". In english this is done by having a subject, a complement and sometimes the use of prepositions. In this language, relations definitions describes a "place structure" with numbered places. By filling those places, assertions can be made.

cti'a fa mi fe plie

I eat an apple

cti'a
x1 eats x2
fa
tags 1st predicate place
mi
x1 is the speaker(s)
fe
tags 2st predicate place
plie
x1 is an apple of species/strain x2

cti'a is a relation word meaning "x1 eats x2". In this definition, x1 represents the place of the one who eats, while x2 represents the place of what is eaten. fa and fe are particles which tags respectively the 1st and 2nd place, and both are followed by the thing which fills them.

The things filling the places are themselves relations. When a relation fills a place, the concept expressed by its 1st place is used. For mi it is "I, the speaker" while with plie it is "an apple". It is thus "I, the speaker" who eats, and "an apple" which is eaten.
