An in depth tour of eberban

Introduction

eberban is a lojban-inspired language aimed at try to change some aspects of it which may be seen as drawbacks, while also experimenting with new ideas.

One of the key aspect of the language is to manipulate only predicates and propositions. Predicates describes the interaction between some entities, while a proposition assert the predicate between the provided entities being true.

Core grammar

Predicates and propositions

The language is made of 2 kinds of things : predicate and propositions. Predicates describes the interaction between some entities, while a proposition asserts the predicate between the provided entities is true. In the english sentence "I eat an apple", there is the predicate of "eating", with the subject being "I" and the thing being eaten is "an apple". This is done by having a subject, a complement and sometimes the use of prepositions. In this language, predicates definitions describes a "place structure" with numbered places. By filling those places, assertions can be made.

cti'a fa mi fe plie

I eat an apple

cti'a
x1 eats x2
fa
tags 1st proposition place
mi
x1 is the speaker(s)
fe
tags 2st proposition place
plie
x1 is an apple of species/strain x2

cti'a is a predicate word meaning "x1 eats x2". In this definition, x1 represents the place of the one who eats, while x2 represents the place of what is eaten. fa and fe are particles which tags respectively the 1st and 2nd place, and both are followed by the thing which fills them.

The things filling the places are themselves predicates. When a predicate fills a place, the concept expressed by its 1st place is used. For mi it is "I, the speaker" while with plie it is "an apple". It is thus "I, the speaker" who eats, and "an apple" which is eaten.
